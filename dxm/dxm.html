UX Design Internship at Baidu

2018

Internship

As an intern interaction designer at Duxiaoman Finance, Baidu Inc. in Beijing, I worked on optimizing the investment product page of the personal finance app. The overall goal was to cater to the needs and habits of different user groups and optimize the arrangement of the product listing on the page so the users will have a smoother experience.

COMPETITOR ANALYSIS

USER RESEARCH

Basing on the user research reports, I located two major user groups of the app and clarified their characteristics and needs. User group A includes young working class users who have limited investing experience and disposable income and who need simple financing solutions. User group B are middle class users who have some experience in risk investment and have a need to keep up with the market and gain more profit.

DESIGN PROCESS

The first step was rearranging the modules on the home page and reduce unnecessary repeating items. Each module now represent a different approach to selecting investment products, such as basing on experience level, strategies, and investment goals.

In the second iteration, I focused more on managing the customer flow and guide users to the specific content according to their experience level. When the user enter the tab for the first time, a popup will suggest that the user reads the newbie guide. Then in the main page, their would be a module for suggested investment products generated by the system based on the user's status.

I got some feedback from the team and gathered some problems to improve. The popup page didn't differentiate the user's log in status. The result of each choice needs to be clarified. Users could use more targeted assistance in choosing products. And the content density could be adjusted in some parts.

To signify the smart algorithm of the platform, I integrating DuXiaoman's AI character into the product recommendation, conveying the message that the suggested products are selected for each user based on their experience level and needs.

Through these iterations, I developed a better understanding of what is expected and needed by users in different phases and learned to construct a coherent and comprehensive user flow by analysing their needs in each scenario.
