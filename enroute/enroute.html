EN ROUTE

Travel your way.

2019

Individual Project

Product Design | UX/UI

En Route is a tool that makes travel planning and route tracking easy and enjoyable. With maximum customizability, the user will be able to plan a route that covers any important spot they wish to visit, along with other activities that satisfy their needs and desires. When the user is on their trip, the app will manage real-time updates according to where they are on their route, and provide insider tips and reminders along the way.

RESEARCH & IDEATION

The target user is travelers who have certain needs and preferences in their trips. They have different priorities in mind depending on the destination, time and people. And they want to make practical and informed travel plans and get the most out of their experience.

The project has a focus on the onboarding experience of a "new arrivial". And I'm seeing it as an oppoturnity to develop a method for people to onboard a travel destination. Starting from there, I brainstormed some ideas about what could help people have the experience they desire in their travels in terms of planning and guiding.

SKETCH & PROTOTYPE

I decided to build a tool that facilitates people to make highly customizable plans according to their preferences and back it up with smart algorithms and a strong knowledge base. While people are traveling, it will become an informed guide for the traveler to refer to.

In the planning phase, the traveler's needs are broken down to different parameters, and the user could be as specific as they wish about their preferences. A suggested route will be generated based on their input. Each spot on the route is changeble individually so the user could explore different options while keeping the spots that they like.

At this point, I got some feedback. It was suggested that there could be two touch points for this tool - a web version that allows the user to give more input and view the wide range of choices, and the mobile version optimized for quick planning and guide on-the-go.

People also said that it would be convenient if the app could document the trip. Based on these feedback, I iterated my idea and developed some prototypes.

ANIMATION & MOTION DESIGN

I created some animation to fill in the transition between the user's input and the system-generated route to give the user a better idea of what's been taken into consideration. I also designed animation that displays when the user reload for a different result. Having the extra time between the input and output not only allows time for the algorithm to calculate in a technical sense, but also communicates to the user that the system is treating every demand carefully.

Mock-up source material on homepage by Anthony Boyd.

Lessons Learned

1. Different touchpoints could be used to cater to the needs of the user in different phases of their experience. Using the web platform for information-heavy scenarios and mobile version for quick data retrival could make it more efficient and easier to maneuver for the user.

2. When designing the user interface for collecting the user's input, the input method(typing, selecting, sliding bar, etc.) should match the type of information, helping the user to understand the indication of their response and avoiding confusions.
