En Route

Time Frame: March 2019 — April 2019
My Role: Product Designer (Independent Project)

En Route is a tool that makes travel planning and route tracking easy and enjoyable. With maximum customizability, the user will be able to plan a route that covers any important spot they wish to visit, along with other activities that meet their interests. When the user is on their trip, the app will manage real-time updates according to where they are on their route, and provide handy tips and reminders along the way.

Problem

It takes a lot of time and effort to plan a trip that covers all your interests in a destination and match your travel style, time, and budget. It's also challenging to keep all the resources handy during the trip. Sometimes unexpected changes happen while you’re on the trip, and it’s not easy to make quick adjustments.

Solution

En Route has a trip planning system that allows users to input their preferences and constraints, and help users build custom itineraries that meet their needs.
The live-tracking function makes it easy for users to stay informed and prepared for their upcoming destinations during the trip.

RESEARCH & IDEATION

Based on the problems defined in this project, I created a proto-persona and a journey map to identify target users' pain points and design opportunities.

The target user is travelers who want an easier way to make travel plans. They might have some interests and priorities in mind as well as some restrictions around time and budget. And they want to make practical and informed travel plans and get the most out of their experience. During they trip, the would like to keep their itinerary handy and be able to make impromptu adjustments if needed.

Through brainstorm, I came up with some features that would help the user make a plan without painstaking research and arrangement but still meet their interests and restrictions.

  • Allow users to input their traveling preferences and limitations (style, pace, duration, budget, must-include destinations, etc.) in the planning interface.
  • Automatically generate a travel plan based on users’ input.
  • Enable users to reorder stops in the trip plan and swap out unwanted destinations.
  • Track users’ progress during the trip and provide live tips and updates.

DESIGN & PROTOTYPE

To help users make highly customizable plans according to their preferences, the app needs a strong knowledge base and powerful algorithms. And the user interface for the trip planner needs to let users access the rich information intuitively.

Once the traveler is on their way, they can launch the planned trip, and the app would serve as an informed travel guide that they can refer to for any information and resources. During this project, I focused on designing the experience of making travel plans and launching them for live-tracking.

In the planning phase, the traveler's needs are broken down to different parameters, and the user could be as specific as they wish about their preferences. A suggested route will be generated based on the user's input. Each spot on the route can be edited so the user could explore different options.

In addition to the mobile app, I also designed a web version, which allows users to take advantage of the screen space and navigate more freely, especially in the planning phase.

During the trip, the user can get information and tips about destinations. Some steps or destinations will also allow users to jump to other relevant apps like Uber, Airbnb, and Yelp for extended functions. If a change to the plan comes up, or an unexpected situation like a bad weather is detected, the app will offer suggestions to adjust the plan.

ANIMATION & MOTION DESIGN

I created some animation to fill in the transition between the user's input and the system-generated route to give the user a better idea of what's been taken into consideration.

I also designed animation that displays when the user reload for a different result. Having the extra time between the input and output not only allows time for the algorithm to calculate in a technical sense, but also communicates to the user that the system is treating every demand carefully.

RETROSPECTIVE

→ Lessons Learned

  • Different touchpoints (devices) could be used to cater to the needs of the user in different phases of their experience. Using the web platform for information-heavy scenarios and mobile version for quick data retrieval could make it more efficient and easier to maneuver for the user.
  • It’s important to strike the right balance between customizability and the amount of information the user needs to input.

→ Areas for Improvement

  • It would be helpful to let users save their preference settings and apply them to future trips.
  • Preference settings on the web interface can be categorized and progressively disclosed, in order to reduce users’ cognitive load.
  • The route tracking capabilities can be expanded on, showing how the system notifies the user of foreseeable changes and responds to plan adjustments.
